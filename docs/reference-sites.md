# Reference Sites

Observed and selected on April 20, 2026. These are direction references, not templates. Borrow principles, not layouts.

## 1. Linear
- URL: https://linear.app
- Borrow:
  - crisp hierarchy and confident spacing
  - strong product framing with minimal clutter
  - motion that feels purposeful, not decorative
- Avoid:
  - cloning the ultra-productized software aesthetic too literally
  - making the site feel like a startup tool when the buyer is a service business owner

## 2. Raycast
- URL: https://www.raycast.com/
- Borrow:
  - obsessive polish and restraint
  - memorable hero composition with a clear point of view
  - excellent rhythm between headline, proof, and visual demo
- Avoid:
  - over-leaning into app-style UI motifs that do not fit a service business site

## 3. Stripe
- URL: https://stripe.com/
- Borrow:
  - premium clarity at scale
  - modular page construction with clear section purpose
  - strong contrast between explanation, proof, and CTA blocks
- Avoid:
  - copying Stripe's gradient language or trying to mimic its financial-tech tone

## 4. Ramp
- URL: https://ramp.com/
- Borrow:
  - sharp conversion focus
  - confident value framing above the fold
  - strong use of proof and action-oriented section transitions
- Avoid:
  - the louder SaaS energy where it would undermine trust for smaller local-business buyers

## 5. Instrument
- URL: https://www.instrument.com/
- Borrow:
  - editorial agency confidence
  - a more design-led but still grown-up visual voice
  - the sense that every section is art-directed
- Avoid:
  - making the site too abstract or too agency-fashion-oriented for a practical business buyer

## 6. Basecamp
- URL: https://basecamp.com/
- Borrow:
  - direct, plainspoken copy structure
  - strong trust through clarity instead of hype
  - landing pages that sell through conviction rather than flash
- Avoid:
  - going too plain or under-designed; this project still needs premium frontend taste

## 7. Framer
- URL: https://www.framer.com/
- Borrow:
  - high-end visual finish
  - sophisticated product framing and interaction polish
  - a good bar for modern web execution quality
- Avoid:
  - overproduced motion or a designer-tool aesthetic that distracts from the service offer

## Reference synthesis
- Desired mix:
  - Linear and Raycast for polish and restraint
  - Instrument for art direction and composition
  - Basecamp for plainspoken selling
  - Stripe and Ramp for structured conversion flow
- Avoid producing a site that looks like a derivative startup SaaS homepage.
- Prefer a clean editorial service-business site with a modern product-quality finish.
