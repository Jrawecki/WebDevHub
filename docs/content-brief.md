# Content Brief

## Positioning
- Lead with websites for service businesses.
- Present client portals and lightweight internal tools as the natural second-step offer after a website engagement.
- Treat broader custom app work as discovery-led, not as an equal homepage promise.

## Audience
- Primary buyer: service-business owners and operators in your state or region.
- Buyer mindset:
  - wants a site that looks credible and modern
  - wants leads and clarity, not design theater
  - may later need intake, booking, portals, or simple internal workflow tooling

## Brand stance
- Hybrid personal-plus-studio branding in v1.
- Use your own name, professional photo, and direct voice as the primary trust anchors while the studio layer remains a placeholder.
- The studio name can become more prominent later, but v1 should not pretend that brand naming is final.
- The tone should be clear, calm, confident, and practical.

## Offer hierarchy
### 1. Website projects
- Primary entry offer.
- Focus on credibility, conversion, clarity, mobile quality, and launch execution.

### 2. Client portals and internal tools
- Secondary offer.
- Position as follow-on work for businesses that need better workflows after the website foundation is in place.

### 3. Ongoing support
- Third offer.
- Position around maintenance, edits, bug fixes, vendor coordination, and small improvements.

## Trust strategy
- Rely first on your expertise, process, and quality bar.
- Use in-progress or concept work honestly with visible status labels.
- Do not make inflated outcome claims you cannot support yet.

## Conversion strategy
- Primary CTA: visible email address.
- Keep contact email-only in v1. Do not add a contact form yet.
- The site should make the next step feel simple and low-risk.
- Requests that are vague or app-heavy should be framed toward paid discovery.

## Page-level messaging goals
### Home
- Answer who you help, what you build, and why it matters within the first screen.

### Services
- Make the offers easy to buy.
- Explain outcomes, not tooling.

### Work
- Show story-based case studies, not a gallery.
- Include current status when projects are still in progress.

### Process
- Reduce buyer uncertainty.
- Show how projects move from discovery to launch.

### About
- Build trust in you as a solo operator.
- Explain your approach and why clients benefit from working directly with the builder.

### Contact
- Keep the contact path simple.
- Explain what happens after someone emails and set a realistic response expectation.
- Keep the visible email address as the only v1 contact method.

## Copy rules
- No hype-heavy startup language.
- No empty claims about innovation, disruption, or enterprise-grade anything.
- Keep sentences readable and concrete.
- Favor plain English over jargon.
- Write for buyers, not other developers.
